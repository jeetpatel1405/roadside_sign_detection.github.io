Abstract

Roadside shop detection plays a significant role in enhancing urban planning, business discovery, and transportation analytics. This project explores a computer vision-based approach for detecting roadside shops from street-level images using deep learning techniques. The aim is to accurately identify shop front locations and improve the efficiency of automated business detection in urban areas. By leveraging convolutional neural networks (CNNs) and large-scale datasets, this project seeks to provide a scalable solution for automated roadside shop detection. The proposed solution will be evaluated on real-world street-level imagery, similar to the Google Street View dataset.

Introduction

  • Roadside shops are essential elements of urban landscapes, contributing to economic and social dynamics.
  • Accurate identification and localization of these shops from street-level imagery can enhance services like business discovery, urban navigation, and smart city initiatives.
  • This project proposes an advanced CNN-based system for roadside shop detection using the "MultiBox" approach, which takes image pixels as input and directly predicts bounding boxes for shops, eliminating traditional hand-crafted proposal generation phases.
  • The goal is to design and develop an efficient and scalable solution for automatic roadside shop detection.
